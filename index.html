About Machina X Deus

Machina X Deus is an exploration into hardware instruments that generate evolving, complex, or emergent behavior.

Rather than standard synthesis tools which act as simple static utilities, our hardware instruments are built to interact. They drift, mutate, and exhibit instability on their own terms. The performer establishes boundaries; the machine decides the final mathematical path.

Inspired by classical chaos theory and modular design, our systems reflect a balanced co-existence of deterministic laws and chaotic results.

Circuit Schematics & Blocks

1. Butterfly3000 (Lorenz Attractor)

Three active integrators coupled in a self-referential loop. Uses two analog multipliers to implement the cross-multiplication states ($xy$ and $xz$). Generates three continuous bipolar signals: X, Y, and Z.

2. Chua Chua (Chua's Circuit)

Built with two capacitors, an inductor, a standard resistor, and the active non-linear element known as the **Chua Diode** (implemented via a dual op-amp negative impedance converter). Unstable scroll rings trigger audio drones.

3. Double Scroll Attractor

Dual hysteric comparator coupled back into a second-order resonant filter loop. Creates rhythmic trigger bursts that mimic neural packet firing patterns.
